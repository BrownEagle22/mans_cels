Mols

One of the first shopping centers in Latvia. It has been one of the most popular stops on Krasta Street for 20 years. There are even specially designed parking spaces for motorcycles to make it convenient for different riders to visit this place. People come here for food and clothes, as well as to spend time with family and friends in one of the eateries and relax zones. It is a place without which Krasta Street is unthinkable.

On one side is the loud, active Krasta Street, where people drive from the center, from Ķengarags, from Pārdaugava and from other nearby areas. On the other side, there are quiet streets in the suburbs of Moscow, where you can hear the tram rails and the pavement of Maskavas Street from time to time. Perhaps this is what is hidden under the name Mols (eng. pier) - Krasta Street and the Daugava is a restless sea, while the suburbs of Moscow are a peaceful land. Just like the 2 waves that are part of the Mols logo - the blue restless sea and the green peaceful land.
